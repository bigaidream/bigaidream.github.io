Jie Fu (姐夫)

Untypical PhD student with his human-friendly big AI dream. He is now (read: still) enjoying the journey at National University of Singapore (NUS), which is generously supported by NGS. He is fortunately under the supervision of Tat-Seng Chua and Huan Xu, also closely working with Kian Hsiang Low. He received a Research Master's degree in Computer Science (First Class Honours) with scholarship (kindly funded by Ministry of Business, Innovation & Employment) from University of Otago, New Zealand, luckily advised by Brendan McCane and Lubica Benuskova.

He is interested in machine learning. More specifically, his research is focused on Bayesian reasoning, deep neural networks, reinforcement learning and some parts of theoretical physics. He is especially excited about reducing the gap between theoretical and practical algorithms in a principled and efficient manner. Inspired by Ernest Hemingway's six-word novel challenge, his current research in 6 words: deep reinforcement learning: no tuition fees.

He strongly believes that the singularity is near and 2045 Initiative is partially feasible (multiply their estimated time by 2). His medium-term goal is "enriching the set of manipulations applicable to training systems, and building reasoning capabilities from the ground up" - From Machine Learning to Machine Reasoning. In the meanwhile, he agrees that people should "keep an eye on unforeseen terrifying scenarios where the products began to threaten humanity". He somehow also thinks that we should learn how to embrace our humanity to maximize heartificial intelligence.
