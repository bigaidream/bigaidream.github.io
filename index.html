Jie Fu

Jie Fu is now (read: still) enjoying his PhD journey at National University of Singapore (NUS) fortunately under the supervision of Tat-Seng Chua and Huan Xu, and closely working with Kian Hsiang Low, which is generously supported by NGS. He received a Research Master's degree in Computer Science (First Class Honours) with scholarship (kindly funded by Ministry of Science + Innovation) from University of Otago, New Zealand, luckily advised by Brendan McCane and Lubica Benuskova.

He is interested in machine learning (probability, statistics & programming). More specifically, his research is focused on nonparametric Bayesian learning (Gaussian processes) with applications in social media analysis. He is especially excited about reducing the gap between theoretical and practical algorithms in a principled and efficient manner. Inspired by Ernest Hemingway, his current research in 6 words: open-box probabilistic learning: tunes black-box functions.

He strongly believes that the singularity is near and 2045 Initiative is partially feasible (multiply their estimated time by 2). His medium-term goal is to achieve strong AI by "enriching the set of manipulations applicable to training systems, and building reasoning capabilities from the ground up" - From Machine Learning to Machine Reasoning. In the meanwhile, he agrees that people should "keep an eye on unforeseen terrifying scenarios where the products began to threaten humanity".
